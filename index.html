Design Philosophy

Built on presence,
not performance

Scout is designed around a single principle: feeling genuinely there. Not the smartest. Not the flashiest. Just honest, calm, and real enough that it earns a place in your family's life. Scout runs on a dedicated Android device — that old phone sitting in your drawer works perfectly — giving it a permanent, always-on home in your living space rather than an app you open and close.

⊕
Stability First

Every feature waits until the foundation is solid. No regressions. No surprises.

◎
Offline First

Cloud AI is a helper. Scout must work without the internet, always.

⌀
Honest, Not Cheerful

Scout admits uncertainty. It doesn't fake confidence or constant enthusiasm.

◉
Family Safe

Designed to be calm enough for children, pets, and long periods of shared space.

What Scout Can Do

Built and working today

Live
Face Animation

Expressive eyes with iris detail, focus breathing, saccades, idle drift, asymmetric blinking, thinking states, and vergence. More alive than a screen should be.

Live
Speech Recognition

Continuous mic listening with discipline. Watchdog recovery. Beep suppression. Barge-in interruption — say "stop" while Scout is speaking and it stops instantly.

Live
Text to Speech

Natural pacing with human-timed pauses before speaking. Tuned pitch and rate. TTS lockout prevents self-triggering. Utterance lifecycle fully managed.

Live
Intent Routing

ScoutIntentRouter handles time, date, greetings, family facts, downloads, vision queries, and more — without misrouting casual phrases as commands.

Live
Face Detection & Gaze

MLKit camera pipeline. Scout's eyes follow you. Gaze smoothing and hold logic prevents snapping. Scene labeling for vision queries.

Live
Gemini AI (Online)

When connected, Scout uses Gemini for open-ended conversation. Offline-graceful fallback. Gemini is a helper — not the dependency Scout runs on.

Live
Memory System

Five-layer architecture. Working memory, habit store, truth database, proposal sandbox, and reflective layer — all isolated and governed separately.

Live
Teaching & Learning

Tell Scout your name, your wife's name, your son's name, your dog's name. It remembers across sessions using TruthDb — not cloud storage.

Planned

TinyLlama or Llama integration. WikDict, WordNet, idiom packs, sentiment. Scout should be genuinely smart when the internet is off.

Memory Architecture

Five layers.
All separated. Always.

Scout's memory is not a single database. It is five distinct layers with strict rules governing what can mix, what can be trusted, and what requires your approval before it becomes real.

01
Working Memory

Temporary RAM state. Current conversation context. Cleared each session. Fast, volatile, and never written to permanent storage.

Volatile RAM
02
Habit Store

JSON-based memory with natural decay. Used for repeated patterns and behavioral preferences. Habits fade if not reinforced — just like humans.

JSON + Decay
03
Truth Database

SQLite. Permanent factual memory. Stable truths approved by you. Your name lives here. Your family lives here. This does not decay.

SQLite · Permanent
04
Proposal Sandbox

Potential learned concepts that Scout wants to remember — but won't until you approve them. Nothing enters the truth layer without permission.

Approval Required
05
Reflective Layer

External AI helper suggestions only. Cloud input is read here, considered here, and never allowed to directly modify Scout's identity or core beliefs.

Read-Only · Cloud
Face Engineering

Eyes that feel present

Scout's face is drawn entirely in software — every frame, every blink, every subtle iris movement calculated in real time. The goal is the opposite of a cartoon: quiet, grounded, believably alive.

▸ Radial iris gradient with 28-spoke fiber detail and limbal ring
▸ Asymmetric blinking — each eye leads the other by a random offset
▸ Focus breathing — tiny iris oscillation when locked onto a face
▸ Smooth spring-based gaze tracking with idle drift and micro-saccades
▸ Vergence support — irises converge for near-focus and thinking states
▸ Lower eyelids lift during thinking — like cheeks, not just lids
▸ Subtle eyelid droop when battery is low. Scout gets tired.
▸ Audio wave with hysteresis gate — won't flicker on background noise
▸ Three teal idle dots pulse when listening but the room is quiet

The Long View
Scout is not meant to become the smartest AI.
It is meant to become the one you trust.

The long-term goal is a companion that earns a quiet, permanent place in your home. Local intelligence first. Offline-capable always. Optional mini-PC brain for heavier thinking when needed — but never required. Scout works without the cloud. That is not a feature. That is the foundation.
